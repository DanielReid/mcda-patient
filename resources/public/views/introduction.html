General instructions

We ask you to consider a situation of a medicine to treat a patient with an advanced cancer. Advanced cancers have usually spread from where they started to other parts of the body. This is known as metastatic cancer. But not all advanced cancers are metastatic. For example, some cancers may be considered advanced because they cannot be cured and are life-threatening even though they have not spread to other parts of the body.

To express the effect of a medicine for the person taking it, we will use probabilities of a certain outcome. For example, suppose that we know from previous studies that out of 100 patients taking the treatment 45 are still alive 12 months after the start of the treatment. We then say that the probability of surviving 12 months is 45 out of 100 (or 45%).
