Part 2: trade-off ratios

In the second part of the questionnaire, you will be asked to judge by how much the effect on one outcome needs to be improved to compensate for a worsening in the effect on another outcome by a specific amount.

For example, consider two treatments A and B. Suppose that for treatment A the probability of surviving the first 12 months is 40% and the probability of severe side-effects is 10% and that for treatment B the probability of surviving the first 12 months is 50% and the probability of severe side-effects is 35%.

Your task is to determine whether the increase in the probability of surviving the first 12 months from 40% to 50% is sufficient to offset the increase in the probability of severe side-effects from 10% to 35%.
