Favourable effects of treatment

Favourable effects of treatments can be expressed in terms of how much they increase the time to progression and survival time.

Time to progression is the time that a treatment can delay the growth of cancer. By delaying the growth of cancer, treatments are expected to delay worsening of symptoms and the need for additional treatment for some time.

Survival time refers to the time that a treatment can prolong survival. By prolonging survival, treatments are expected to also delay the growth of cancer and worsening of symptoms and the need for additional treatment for some time.
